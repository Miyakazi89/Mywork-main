Cyber-Attacks Overview

Understanding the landscape of cyber-attacks is crucial to protecting your digital assets. Here, we provide an overview of the most common cyber-attacks targeting individuals and businesses.

Common Attack Types

  • Phishing: Fraudulent attempts to obtain sensitive information by disguising as a trustworthy entity.
  • Malware: Malicious software designed to damage or gain unauthorized access to systems.
  • Ransomware: A type of malware that encrypts data and demands payment for its release.
  • DDoS Attacks: Distributed Denial of Service attacks that overwhelm a system, making it unavailable to users.
  • SQL Injection: Exploiting vulnerabilities in web applications to execute malicious SQL code.

Prevention Tips

  • Use strong, unique passwords and change them regularly.
  • Enable multi-factor authentication (MFA) on all accounts.
  • Keep software and systems updated with the latest security patches.
  • Educate employees about recognizing phishing attempts and other social engineering attacks.
  • Implement robust firewall and antivirus solutions.

Incident Response Steps

  1. Identify: Recognize and confirm the security incident.
  2. Contain: Limit the spread and impact of the incident.
  3. Eradicate: Remove the cause of the incident and affected systems.
  4. Recover: Restore systems and services to normal operation.
  5. Review: Analyze the incident and improve future response strategies.

Common

Find answers to frequently asked questions about cybersecurity and protect your online presence.

What is Cybersecurity? +

Cybersecurity refers to the practice of protecting computers, mobile devices, electronic systems, networks and data from digital attacks or unauthorized access.

Why is Cybersecurity important? +

In today's digital age, cybersecurity is crucial in safeguarding sensitive information and ensuring business operations run smoothly.

How can I protect my online accounts? +

To protect your online accounts, use strong and unique passwords, enable two-factor authentication, and monitor your accounts regularly for suspicious activity.

What should I do if I suspect a cybersecurity threat? +

If you suspect a threat, disconnect from the internet, change your passwords, and seek professional assistance.

How can I improve my cybersecurity? +

Improve your cybersecurity by regularly updating your software, using reliable antivirus programs, and staying informed on the latest threats.
